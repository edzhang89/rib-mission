The Timeline

NOW

Ribs out of the fridge

All 6 racks on sheet pans. Rub's on — just check the membrane is off the bone side of each rack (corner of a paper towel, pull diagonally). Cold ribs onto the smoker is fine; no need to temper.

10:00

Fire the YS640

Set 250°F. Full hopper — 6 hours of cooking burns ~10+ lbs of pellets. Let it settle at temp a full 15 minutes before loading; six cold racks will sag the pit 20–30° and that's fine. Don't chase it — it recovers.

10:30

Six racks on

Bone side down, no overlapping — use the second shelf for 2–3 racks. On the YS640 the firebox side runs hotter, so put the meatier racks there. Stick probe 1 between bones of the thickest rack, probe 2 in a top-shelf rack. Then close the lid and leave it closed.

12:30

Rotate + spritz (one lid opening)

Swap top ↔ bottom shelves and spin racks front-to-back so everything sees equal heat. Quick spritz (apple juice/water 50:50) if the edges look dry. In and out in 3 minutes — every extra minute of open lid costs ~10 of recovery.

1:30

Color checkpoint — wrap decision

Mahogany and set? Ride unwrapped to the finish (better bark). Getting dark fast? Wrap the dark ones in foil with a splash of juice for ~1 hour. With 6 racks it's fine to wrap some and not others.

2:45

Bend test window opens

Pick a rack up with tongs a third from one end — done when it bends ~90° and the bark cracks. Probes reading 195–203°F between bones agrees. Racks will finish at different times; pull each as it passes, hold wrapped in a dry cooler.

3:15

Sauce + set (optional)

Thin coat, back on 15 minutes to tack up. Skip entirely if you're a dry-rub house — the ribs won't miss it.

3:40

Rest

Tent loosely with foil, 15–20 minutes. Slice bone side up so you can see what you're cutting. Single bones for the kids, doubles for adults.

4:00

Serve 🎇

Six racks ≈ 12–15 adult servings. Miller Lite goes in the left hand.

⚠️ Six-Rack Rules

  • Airflow beats position. A half-inch gap between racks matters more than which shelf they're on.
  • Pit dips below 225 for 20+ min? Check the pellet hopper for a bridge/tunnel — stir it.
  • Running late at 2:30? Wrap everything and bump to 275. Foil + higher heat claws back 45 minutes with barely any quality loss.
  • Running early? Ribs hold beautifully — foil, towels, dry cooler, up to 2 hours.
